Pic Hi hi! My path to programming wasn't straight. Like many first generation Asian Americans, I went into college thinking I was going to be a doctor because it was a safe path. After graduating, I was no longer certain that I wanted to be a doctor. Since my family was poor, I couldn't go backpacking in Europe during this time of indecision. So, I began to work temp jobs to pay rent. I chanced upon accounting and worked as an accountant for six years before deciding to study programming. It was risky to quit my job but I am trying to learn to embrace being uncomfortable. Besides coding, I also began to explore something I have never been good at - art. I started drawing more frequently and explored other ways to be creative, such as taking photos and completing paint by numbers.
